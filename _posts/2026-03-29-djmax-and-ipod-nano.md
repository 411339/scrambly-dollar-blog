While **DJMAX RESPECT V** arrived in 2020, bringing high-definition rhythm action to modern platforms, and the **5th generation iPod Nano** debuted in 2007 as Apple's revolutionary color-screen MP3 player, these two icons share a surprising lineage — both embodying the spirit of that transformative era.

## A Brief History Lesson

### DJMAX RESPECT V (2020)

Released by **NEOWIZ** (the Korean developer behind the beloved rhythm franchise), DJMAX RESPECT V brought:

- Full HD music videos
- Online multiplayer mode
- Tracks from artists like Marshmello, Porter Robinson, and Yukika
- Online leaderboards and community challenges
- Modern graphics but classic arcade feel

Available on Steam, Xbox, and PlayStation 4, the game captured the nostalgia of earlier entries while modernizing the experience.

### iPod Nano 5th Generation (2007)

Apple's 5th iPod Nano was a revelation:

- First iPod Nano with **color screen**
- Clickwheel navigation (last of its kind)
- **2GB or 4GB storage**
- **MP3, JPEG, and AAC playback**
- **File management via MTP mode** (no iTunes required)

The 5th Nano was iconic for its sleek, jewel-like design and vibrant colors — pink, blue, black, white.
